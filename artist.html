Top 4 Artist In Malaysia

ETEN TEO

Eten Teo, founder of Redcut Paper Cutting, is a Malaysian-born artist with more than 12 years of paper cutting exeperience.

Skilled in preserving this invaluable culture, Eten continues to create beautiful, unique pieces that combines both traditional and modern elements while injecting his own Malaysian twists.

Eten has been interviewed by Malaysia media, including Astro, 8TV, RTM, BNC Chinese, NTV7, The Star, Melody FM, aifm, Cityplus FM, Sin Chew Daily, China Press, Nanyang Siang Pau, Guang Ming Daily and others. He is also often invited to participate in paper-cutting activities, including KLIA CNY event, Burberry CNY Event, Tory Burch CNY Event, Vivo CNY event, Media Prima Berhad event and other.

FOONG YENG YENG

Yeng Yeng was born on 27/08/1995, a Malaysian Visual Artist in Kuala Lumpur received a professional Diploma in Fine Art from the Malaysian institute of art (MIA) in 2017.

Her passion in art started when she was 5 years old, when she attended an art tuition centre learning the basics. In primary and secondary school years she participated in various art competitions and activities gaining recognition from these institutions.

The experience and explosure of her art acitivies throughout her career, enabled her in building personal relationships with art and to continue as a practicing artist to be inspired with the local artists contemporary society; where multicultural expression sparks fresh perspectives and ideas.

RAY TAN

Tan, 45, discovered his passion for Chinese paper cutting while working at a shopping mall in his early 20s. He observed an artist cutting intricate designs on pieces of red paper for the Lunar New Year using scissors and small knives.

In 2006, he opened his store, Red Handicrafts. Inside, giant lanterns hang from the ceiling, while low tables and racks display his paper cutouts of Chinese zodiacs, postcards, brightly coloured ornaments, and knick-knacks.

Each of his creations is not frivolously put together, but instead carefully designed to clearly and effectively represent Chinese culture.

GAO YI HUA

As leading conservationists of traditional paper-cut, Gao Yihua exhibit works that reflect the rich Chinese cultural context of paper-cutting and its classical beauty with unique folk patterns.

She came from a farming community in Shaanxi Province. At age six, she began learning paper-cutting with her grandmother and mother. She has acquired a unique style of paper-cutting and contributed immensely to the enrichment of folk art culture. Her works have won many awards in international competitions. She is one of the most important preservers of this precious art form.

In 2009, UNESCO listed it in the “Masterpieces of the Oral and Intangible Heritage of Humanity”. Today, contemporary paper-cutting is active in the international art scene.
