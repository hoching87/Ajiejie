剪纸艺术

Chinese paper-cutting is an art form of Chinese cultural heritage that ranges from simple basic designs consisting of a single image to symmetrical designs created by folding the paper into equal parts before cutting. Show design. Forming a symmetrical design, usually he is folded an even number of times, such as 2 or 4 times.

These cut-paper decorations are often glued to the exterior of windows, so the light from the inside shines through the negative space of the cutout.Usually, the artworks are made of red paper, as red is associated with festivities and luck in Chinese culture, but other colours are also used. Normally cut-paper artwork is used on festivals such as Chinese New Year, weddings and childbirth, as cut-paper artwork is considered to symbolize luck and happiness.

    ORIGIN OF CULTURE

The oldest surviving paper cut out is a symmetrical circle from the 6th century found in Xinjiang, China.However, long before paper was invented, there have been records of the Chinese using other thin materials such as leaves, silver or gold foil, silk, and leather to carve hollowed patterns. It was found that during the Western Zhou Dynasty (1046 – 771 BC).
